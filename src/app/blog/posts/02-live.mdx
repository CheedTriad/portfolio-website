"It's done!" - Batman (2006 -probably)

Well, "done" in the way that software is ever done. Hehe. It looks good, and I have decided to stop touching it before I break something 
that was working yesterday.

Right. After a week of locking in, debugging Vercel builds at midnight, learning that 
TypeScript has opinions about my pixel values, accidentally creating five identical 
Vercel projects, and at one point spending forty minutes on a spacing issue that 
not even Einstein would spot,the portfolio is [now live!!!](https://chidiereoluoma.com).

## What's on the site

The main portfolio covers my work in data and business analysis, my projects, 
and a bit about who I am. There's also a separate section for my music work — 
organ and piano for weddings, funerals, and concerts — because apparently my fingers love keyboards in all shapes and types. 

Pages that made it in:

- **Home**:- the headline, the aura, the feng shui (yes, I am Gen-Z. go figure)
- **About**:- work history, skills, education, and interests
- **Projects**:- well, my... projects, of course! 
- **Blog**:- you're reading it  *( ͡° ͜ʖ ͡°)* 
- **Organist**:- repertoire, biography, and a booking form
- **Gallery**:- my photobook, shot over the last decade on cameras of varying dignity (you don't need to be told that my iPhone 11 doesn't rank very high on that scale)

## What's next

We're live but the work doesn't stop here. Next up:

**Graduate Job Market Analyser**:- It is a Python-based tool that scrapes live 
graduate job listings, extracts the most in-demand skills, salary ranges, 
and locations, and surfaces the trends in an interactive dashboard. 
I am building this, partly because I need it, partly because the data is genuinely 
interesting, and partly to prove a point about what I can 
ship in a week or two.
