*-Clears throat-*

I assumed that pushing the remaining bit of code for my final year project, and turning in my final case study report (previous blog post if you havent seen) would have a greater sense of relief.
Don't get me wrong, it does feel incredibly good to have closed that chapter, but it also feels a little bit like... nothing? Like, I know that the work is done, but it doesn't feel like the work is done. 

I reflected on my entire journey to get this degree, and it does get me a bit emotional when I remember how much effort myself, my family and my friends have put into this. I still remember the first day I arrived in Leicester, and how tired I was. I sat at the edge of the bare mattress of my empty room, and told myself "alright, Chidi, it's just another new beginning. You're used to these by now."
Maybe I was. Maybe that was why I didn't feel as nervous as I expected. But I also think that the fact that I had no expectations for how this would go, and no expectations for how I would feel, meant that I was just... open to whatever came. And it came in the form of a lot of hard work, a lot of late nights, a lot of learning, a lot of growth, and a lot of fun.

Alot has happened in the last four years. I have seen parts of the world I never thought I would see, met people I never thought I would meet, and learned things I never thought I would...  well... learn. I'm sure you get the gist. I conquered fears, experienced life in a new way, loved, and also pursued dreams that I thought were unattainable.
I am grateful for every single moment of this journey, and I am grateful for everyone who has been a part of it, especially my lovely parents and my amazing siblings.

Now, the sappy bit's out of the way, I am really excited for what comes next. I have prayed, and made a lot of plans, and I am really looking forward to the next chapter. Before you ask me about getting a Masters degree, just know the last guy who asked is still missing 😐.

Maybe, just maybe, I will write a blog post about what comes next. But for now, I will continue working, cycling and enjoying the summer. 

Till then, stay safe and hydrated 😉.


With love,

-Chidi
