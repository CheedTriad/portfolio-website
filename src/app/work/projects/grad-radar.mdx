## Overview

GradRadar started as a practical question: "What does the UK data job market 
actually look like from the inside?" Not career advice, not generic LinkedIn 
posts, but real data. 635 genuine job listings from Glassdoor UK, cleaned, 
analysed, and turned into something anyone can interact with.

The python-built dashboard lets you filter by country, work type, and salary range, and 
immediately see how the market shifts.

## What it analyses

- **Most in-demand skills**:- ranked by frequency across all listings. 
  Machine Learning and SQL lead by a significant margin.
- **Skill combinations**:- which skills appear together most often, 
  revealing what a complete profile actually looks like to employers.
- **Salary distribution**:- a full histogram of what the market pays, 
  from entry-level to senior.
- **Salary by city**:- which UK cities pay most for data roles.
- **Remote vs onsite split**:- how much of the market is genuinely flexible.
- **Top hiring companies**:- who is actually recruiting, and how many 
  roles they're advertising.
- **Company rating vs salary**:- does a better-rated employer pay more? 
  The scatter plot answers that directly.
- **Country breakdown**:- England, Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland 
  compared.

## Tech stack

- **Python**:- data processing and analysis
- **Pandas**:- cleaning, filtering, and transformation
- **Plotly**:- all interactive charts
- **Streamlit**:- dashboard interface and deployment
- **Kaggle**:- data source (Glassdoor UK listings, 2024)

## Why I built it

I am a final-year Computer Science student actively applying for graduate 
roles. The job market feels opaque when you're inside it. You apply, you 
wait, and you wonder whether you're even targeting the right skills. GradRadar 
was my attempt to remove some of that uncertainty, for myself and for anyone 
else in the same position.

The insight that surprised me most: Machine Learning appears in nearly three 
times as many listings as the third-ranked skill. If you're targeting UK data 
roles and haven't touched ML, the market is telling you something.

## Outcome

A fully deployed, publicly accessible data product built and shipped in a 
single session. The live dashboard is available at the link above, with no login 
required. It's fully interactive and free to use.
